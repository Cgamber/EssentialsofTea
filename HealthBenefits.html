Tea has been consumed for centuries and is known for its health benefits. Here is an overview of some of the health benefits of different types of teas, along with some sources for further reading: Green tea - Green tea is known for its high levels of antioxidants, which can help protect cells from damage and reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as cancer and heart disease (1). Green tea has also been shown to improve brain function, boost metabolism, and promote weight loss (2). Black tea - Black tea has been shown to improve heart health by reducing the risk of heart disease and stroke (3). Black tea is also rich in antioxidants and has been linked to lower levels of stress hormones and improved mental alertness (4). White tea - White tea has the highest levels of antioxidants of all the teas and has been shown to have anti-aging effects on the skin (5). White tea may also help reduce the risk of heart disease and lower blood pressure (6). Herbal tea - Herbal teas, such as chamomile and peppermint, have a range of health benefits. Chamomile tea has been shown to reduce inflammation and improve sleep quality (7), while peppermint tea may help relieve digestive issues such as bloating and constipation (8). Oolong tea - Oolong tea is a traditional Chinese tea that is believed to have weight loss and metabolism-boosting properties (9). Oolong tea may also help reduce the risk of heart disease and improve bone health (10). Overall, tea is a healthy beverage choice that offers a range of health benefits. However, it is important to keep in mind that individual results may vary, and tea should not be relied upon as a sole treatment for any medical condition.
